One step at a time

Matthew Sanetra November 28, 2022

Time feels like its speeding up exponentially fast. My weeks feel as if they go by 10x as fast as they did even just a year ago. And those felt like they went by twice as fast as the year before that. Last year, I told myself that it was because of the pandemic, but now I believe that it's because of workload and stress.

I'm an Oxford student, and I knew what I was signing myself up for. But no matter how prestigious my current situation is, I still think "why why whyy didn't I go to a different university?". It's definitely a rhetorical question, but in answering, I think I will be able to share a lot about me, considering how much context such a decision needs.

One can dream

In September 2021, when applying to university, I realised I have a chance at Oxford. It was pure luck that the combination of subjects that I did at A-level were the most common ones amongst CS students. I did not think about any potential degrees at all when picking my subjects. I just chose what I liked.

I don't remember much about the application process, it seems as if I was just on an autopilot going through life. My Oxford application was exactly the same as my other university applications, except I also had to register for the Mathematics Admissions Test (MAT). I didn't have anyone to help me except my school - none of my parents went to university, let alone know the process for Oxford.

I'm a procrastinator - I didn't even know what the questions were like on the exam until 2 weeks prior to it. All I knew before that point was that you essentially only needed the AS-level maths syllabus1 (which I completed almost a whole year prior) and to aim for around 60/100 to get an interview2. I did three past papers in exam conditions and I averaged 55/100. At this point, I don't remember why, but I didn't take Oxford too seriously, and somewhat gave up, thinking whatever will happen, will happen. I didn't do any more practice.

I left the exam almost crying. I knew I got almost no marks at all in the actual Computer Science logic questions - all my marks came from the Mathematics section. That year's paper was much harder than the three I previously attempted. When I finally got round to estimating what I got (the exam was released shortly after), it calmed me down because I estimated ~50. Until there was radio silence while other students were invited to interviews.

It is now December, and still radio silence. And then finally - in the span of a day, I was invited to not one, two or three - but FOUR interviews! When I shared this news with my school's careers team, they immediately set out to help me prepare.

My first interview was with one of my current professors, Rahul. It went so much better than I expected it to, and we both still remember it. I remember him to be very kind and I actually had a lot of fun, so considering that an Oxford interview is actually meant to resemble a tutorial, it made me realise the true potential of a degree there. But my other three interviews went bad. Very bad. I once again lost hope.

The biggest surprise of my life

11th January, it is Oxbridge decision day. I got my offer. I was officially one of 47 students who got an offer to study Computer Science at Oxford3. It was surreal. (I also got my MAT score back - 45. Offer holder average was 61.73.)

Now's the time I had a clear choice of whether I go to Oxford, or not. I never expected to get so far, and everyone was congratulating me. Everyone wanted me to go. I personally didn't believe I had what it takes to study there, but I went for it in hopes that I was being harsh on myself - I did get in without trying, after all. And here I am, at the end of the first trimester, struggling, and procrastinating by starting a blog.

Final thoughts

Don't get me wrong - I love it here and it is genuinely great. It's my home and I feel that I belong here. Everything is perfect, except I am currently in the process of getting up to speed, something which I've seen everyone struggle with at the beginning - I'm just taking a bit more time.

Everyone is supportive of me, my tutors are great with offering me more help, and I get free counselling.

I just wish I had more free time. □

- Matthew

Disclaimer

After writing this article, I realised that some may think that the only reason I got in is because I was privileged. I was the only one from my year at my school that ended up going to Oxford as an undergrad - another student started an Oxford foundation year. My school (grade-wise) was underperforming.
